Carl Fredricksen

~ About Me ~

As a young boy, Carl Fredricksen idolized explorer Charles Muntz. Carl met fellow Muntz fan Ellie, they bonded over this, and their relationship blossomed. Ellie had a "clubhouse"—an abandoned house in the neighborhood, and when the two later married they lived in that house which they rebuilt. Carl worked as a balloon salesman and Ellie was a tour guide at the zoo. Ellie always dreamed of moving their house to a place called Paradise Falls in South America, and so Carl and her started saving up for a trip to go there. Unfortunately, when the trip was coming up, Ellie fell ill and died soon after. Living a pretty simple life, Carl fondly talks about his most memorable adventure.
After he retired, Carl decided to keep Ellie's promise, and so he turned his house into a makeshift airship using countless helium balloons and flying away. By accident, he was joined by Russell, an eight-year-old "Wilderness Explorer" who visited Carl in an effort to earn his final merit badge for assisting the elderly.
They embarked on an adventure hoping to reach the falls before the balloons deflated. But they took on another mission on their way when they met a giant, colorful flightless bird, whom they named Kevin. Kevin was in danger and needed help to reunite with her chicks. Along the way they met a dog named Dug who joined their journey.
After some turmoil, they managed to complete their mission and returned Kevin home, and also get the house to Paradise Falls.
After that Carl and Russel returned home. Russell finally received his "Assisting the Elderly" badge, and from then Carl lives calmly in an elderly home knowing his wife's dream came true.
